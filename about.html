Vishvakarma is known as the divine architect and craftsman in Hindu mythology. He is regarded as the creator of the universe, as well as the designer of heavenly palaces, weapons, and tools for the gods. He is also considered the god of carpenters, artisans, engineers, and craftsmen.

He built the cities and palaces for gods, including Dwarka (Lord Krishna’s city), Indraprastha (the Pandavas’ capital), and Lanka (the kingdom of Ravana).

Vishvakarma Puja is celebrated by craftsmen and workers, especially in factories and workshops, where they pray for their tools and seek blessings for their work. It usually takes place in September or October.
